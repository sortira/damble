How it works

  1. 1
    Gather 2+ players — everyone turns their camera on.
  2. 2
    Deal a card on your turn — or gamble: edit the joke and bet on it.
  3. 3
    Everyone else resists. Their webcam measures the laughter on-device.
  4. 4
    You score the average giggle you provoke. Straightest face wins ties.

How to play Damble

The idea

Damble is a laughing standoff. Everyone joins a table with their camera on. On your turn you deal one of your dad-joke cards to the table — and try to make everyone else crack up while they try to stay stone-faced.

Scoring

When you deal, each other player's browser measures their laughter across a short window and reports the average intensity (0–100%). You score 10 × average from every player who laughs. Those points are also added to each laugher's “conceded” tally.

Hiding your face or looking away reads as not laughing — so it lowers your reaction (and the host can remove anyone who won't play fair).

The gamble

Feeling bold? Instead of dealing a card as-is, edit the joke and place a bet (1–50 pts):

  • Provoke at least your bet in laughs → you earn double the points.
  • Fall short → you lose your bet from your tally.

High risk, high reward. Rewrite grandma's favourite into comedy gold, or crash and burn.

Winning

After everyone has dealt all their cards, the highest score wins. Ties break in favour of whoever conceded the fewest points — the coolest customer at the table.

Controls

  • Create / Join a table with a 4-letter code.
  • Host can start the game and kick players.
  • Rejoin — if you drop out or refresh, a Rejoin button restores your seat.
  • Theme — toggle light / dark in the top-right.

About

Damble — a web game where the only way to win is to keep a straight face.

Who made it

Damble is a side project by Aritro Shome (sortira), a student research engineer working on AI interpretability & alignment — currently researching circuit tracing at AI4Bharat, with interests spanning reasoning models, reinforcement learning, synthetic data, and edge AI.

Why it exists

Most of that work lives inside the model. Damble is the opposite end of the stack: a deliberate playground for exploring system internals and software-engineering concepts hands-on, end to end. Building a silly party game turns out to be a great excuse to wrestle with the real stuff:

  • Real-time multiplayer over WebSockets, with an authoritative server-side state machine as the single source of truth.
  • Concurrency — reaction windows, timeouts, and idempotent round resolution under an async event loop.
  • On-device ML inference (face-api.js on TensorFlow.js) so video never leaves the browser and the server stays cheap.
  • Session identity & reconnect, host moderation, and graceful failure handling for the messy realities of live connections.
  • Cost-conscious design that fits a free-tier instance — social presence via tiny messages instead of streaming video.

The full breakdown lives in DESIGN.md for anyone who wants to study how the pieces fit together.
